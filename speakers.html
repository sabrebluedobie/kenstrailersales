Eugenia Bettis

Office Manager

A great office manager is not just someone who provides all of the amenities for our employees, she emphasizes the unwritten rules, behavior, beliefs, and the motivations that enable good work to get done. However, a bad manager can be what stifles a workforce.

In Eugenia’s office, she has shown skills with budgeting, computer programs, data entry, decision-making, conflict resolution, multi-tasking, as well as being detail-oriented. She has modeled how to recognize the traits of organizations that *get* the big picture. She can share what those teams, departments, and companies do that others don’t. Once you’ve worked within a culture of good management, it’s almost impossible to imagine yourself anywhere else.
